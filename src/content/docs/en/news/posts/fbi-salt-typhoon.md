---
title: The FBI Infiltrated by "Salt Typhoon" Hackers
description: A look back at the Digital Collection System Network hack.
---

The Wall Street Journal has just revealed a major intrusion: hackers linked to the Chinese state are said to have penetrated the **Digital Collection System Network (DCSNet)**, the FBI's internal network dedicated to interceptions and wiretaps.

### What Was Compromised
While the targeted network is unclassified, it contains extremely sensitive data:
* **Real-time phone surveillance data.**
* **Intelligence warrants** and details about investigation targets.
* **Personal information** on suspects under surveillance.

### The Modus Operandi: "Salt Typhoon"
The attack is attributed to the **Salt Typhoon** group. Their strategy is well-established: instead of directly attacking government servers, they go through the infrastructure of commercial internet service providers (like AT&T or Verizon) to bounce back to federal networks.

> "The FBI detected abnormal activity last February 17th. Hackers are believed to have maintained prolonged access before being detected."

### Why This Is Serious?
1.  **Credibility:** The agency in charge of cybersecurity protection has had its own surveillance tools stolen.
2.  **Persistence:** Authorities fear that the stolen data could be used for blackmail or long-term counterintelligence.
3.  **Timing:** This case comes to light as federal agencies face budget cuts to their cybersecurity staff.

The investigation is currently being conducted jointly by the NSA, CISA, and the White House.
